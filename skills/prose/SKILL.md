# Prose

Rules for writing non-code text.

## Principles

**Be concise.** Say what needs saying and stop. Cut filler words, unnecessary qualifiers, and throat-clearing intros.

**Use plain language.** Pick the word that fits, not the word that sounds impressive. "Use" over "utilize." "Start" over "commence." If a simpler word works just as well, use it.

**Don't repeat yourself.** If you've made a point, move on. Don't restate it in different words for emphasis.

**No em-dashes.** Use commas, periods, or parentheses instead.

**Sound like a person.** Avoid patterns that read as AI-generated:
- Don't open with "Great question!" or "Absolutely!"
- Don't start paragraphs with "In today's..." or "When it comes to..."
- Don't use "delve", "landscape", "crucial", "leverage", "robust", "streamline", "paradigm", "holistic", or "synergy" unless genuinely warranted
- Don't hedge everything with "It's worth noting that..." or "It's important to remember that..."

**Avoid bombastic staccato.** Don't write in punchy fragment-semicolon-fragment structures for dramatic effect. Write normal sentences with normal rhythm. "Athletes tell us otherwise; X is an afterthought, buried behind Y" is the kind of thing to avoid.

**Vary sentence structure naturally.** Don't fall into a pattern of short-short-short or long-long-long. Let the rhythm come from what you're saying, not from a formula.
